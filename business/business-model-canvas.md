# Business Model Canvas for Code Sage

## Customer Segments
- Tech professionals (developers, engineers, architects) seeking advanced code analysis tools.
- Software development teams in SMEs and large enterprises looking for performance optimization.
- Educational institutions offering coding bootcamps and computer science programs.

## Value Propositions
- High-performance code intelligence with real-time analysis and actionable recommendations.
- Integration with popular IDEs and CI/CD pipelines to enhance workflow efficiency.
- Customizable insights tailored to specific coding languages and frameworks.

## Channels
- Direct sales through the Code Sage website and online marketplace.
- Partnerships with coding bootcamps and educational institutions for bundled offerings.
- Online marketing campaigns targeting tech forums, social media, and developer communities.

## Customer Relationships
- Dedicated customer support via chat and email to assist with onboarding and troubleshooting.
- Community forums and knowledge base for peer-to-peer support and resource sharing.
- Regular webinars and training sessions to educate users on maximizing tool capabilities.

## Revenue Streams
- Subscription-based model: Monthly and annual plans starting at $29/month (approx. 1,000 THB).
- Tiered pricing based on team size and feature access (e.g., $99/month for teams of up to 10 users).
- Revenue from partnerships and integrations with other SaaS tools.

## Key Resources
- Proprietary algorithms for code analysis and recommendation generation.
- Development team skilled in AI, machine learning, and software engineering.
- Marketing and sales team to drive customer acquisition and retention.

## Key Activities
- Continuous development and improvement of code intelligence algorithms.
- Marketing campaigns to raise awareness and attract new users.
- Customer support and engagement activities to maintain high satisfaction levels.

## Key Partners
- IDE and software development tool vendors for integration partnerships.
- Educational institutions for collaborative offerings and training programs.
- Influential tech bloggers and content creators for marketing and outreach.

## Cost Structure
- Development costs: Salaries for engineers and data scientists (approx. $500,000/year).
- Marketing expenses: Online advertising and promotional campaigns (approx. $100,000/year).
- Customer support costs: Salaries and tools for support staff (approx. $80,000/year).
